Use strong, unique passwords: Create passwords with 12+ characters mixing letters, numbers, and symbols (e.g., "BhopalCS2026!Hack"). Use a password manager app like LastPass or Bitwarden, and enable two-factor authentication (2FA) on email, college portals, and social media.
Avoid public Wi-Fi for sensitive tasks: Skip college cafe or hostel Wi-Fi for banking or logins. Use mobile data or a VPN app like ExpressVPN to encrypt your connection.
Keep apps and devices updated: Turn on auto-updates for your phone, laptop, and apps. This patches security holes—check Settings > Software Update weekly.
Think before sharing personal info: Don't post Aadhaar, PAN, or college ID on social media. Set profiles to private and limit friend requests from unknowns.
Spot phishing scams: Ignore unsolicited calls, SMS, or emails asking for OTPs or "verify your account." Hover over links to check URLs before clicking—legit ones won't rush you.
Secure UPI transactions: Always verify the recipient's name and amount before confirming payments on PhonePe, Google Pay, or Paytm. Set a daily UPI limit (e.g., ₹5,000) in app settings and avoid sharing PINs or QR screenshots.
Enable UPI safety features: Use apps' biometric locks (fingerprint/face ID) and virtual payment addresses instead of phone numbers. Report suspicious transactions immediately via the app's "Report" button.
Download only from trusted sources: Stick to Google Play Store or official sites for apps—no sideloading APKs from WhatsApp forwards, which often carry malware.
Backup data regularly: Use Google Drive or college cloud storage for assignments and photos. Encrypt backups to protect against ransomware.
Log out from shared devices: Always sign out of college lab computers or friends' phones after use—especially from email or banking apps.
